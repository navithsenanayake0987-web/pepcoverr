Overview

What is Ipamorelin?

Ipamorelin is a synthetic pentapeptide — just 5 amino acids — that acts as a ghrelin mimetic. It binds to the ghrelin receptor (GHS-R) in the pituitary gland and stimulates the release of growth hormone. Unlike some other GH secretagogues, it is highly selective and does not significantly raise cortisol or prolactin.

Its selectivity is its main advantage over older growth hormone secretagogues like GHRP-2 and GHRP-6. Those peptides were effective but came with cortisol spikes and hunger-stimulating side effects. Ipamorelin produces clean GH pulses without those downsides — which is why it became the preferred option in the community.

It is almost universally stacked with CJC-1295. The combination works through two complementary pathways — CJC-1295 via the GHRH receptor and Ipamorelin via the ghrelin receptor — producing a larger and more sustained GH release than either peptide alone.

Scientific Evidence

What the research actually says

Ipamorelin dose-response — GH release in healthy subjects
Moderate
Journal of Endocrinology · 1998 · Raun et al.
Dose-dependent GH release confirmed in healthy subjects. Ipamorelin was shown to be highly selective — no significant cortisol or prolactin elevation observed. The selectivity profile is better characterized than most peptides in this class.

Ipamorelin in postoperative ileus — Phase IIb trial
Moderate
Gastroenterology · 2013 · Substantial clinical trial
Ipamorelin was actually studied in a proper clinical trial for gastrointestinal motility. Results were mixed — did not meet primary endpoints for postoperative ileus. Shows the peptide reached clinical trial stage, which most experimental peptides never do.

GH secretagogues and body composition — review
Limited
Review · Multiple studies
Broader review of GH secretagogue effects on body composition shows modest improvements in lean mass and fat reduction, but effect sizes are modest compared to direct GH or anabolic agents.

Summary

The bottom line

Ipamorelin is the cleanest GH secretagogue available — its selective mechanism avoids the cortisol and prolactin side effects of older peptides in this class. It actually reached a proper Phase IIb clinical trial (for gastrointestinal use), which puts it in a small group of experimental peptides with real clinical trial experience. The most commonly reported benefit is improved sleep quality. Its combination with CJC-1295 is the gold standard GH peptide stack in the community.
